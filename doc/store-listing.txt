Full description:
Your memory extension. AIS is a fast, private index for the things you want to
find again: links, documents, notes, and passwords, filed under your own
keywords and recalled by any combination of them.

Everything stays on your device in plain text you can read and export; secrets
are encrypted. No accounts, no ads, no tracking, no servers, and your data never
leaves your phone unless you sync it yourself. Optional sync is on-demand and
peer-to-peer: a one-time transfer directly between your own devices over your
local network, end to end encrypted.

Built on a small C engine for instant startup and search over very large
collections, the same engine behind the desktop, web, and command-line versions.

- File anything under your own tags, recall by any combination.
- Plain-text storage you own and can export; encrypted secrets.
- No accounts, no ads, no tracking, no cloud.
- On-demand, end-to-end-encrypted LAN sync between your own devices.
- Fast even at a lifetime of entries.

Your index is plain text, so it outlives its own tools: it survives decades of
archiving, it will still open in 50 years, and you can import it into other
programs whenever you want. No lock-in.

Why that matters: keeping data readable for decades is a genuinely unsolved
problem in computer science, the "digital dark age", where file formats and the
apps that open them die faster than the data. Plain text is the oldest, safest
answer: readable since the 1960s, on any machine, with no special program.
